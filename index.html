The Journey of Ra

In Egyptian myth, the sun god Ra makes a daily journey through the underworld, spanning from sunset in the west and sunrise in the east. The journey is associated with the imagery of continual death and rebirth - the sun "dies" as it sets and is "reborn" when it rises.

Many Egyptian deities assist Ra through this journey as he sails through the 12 kingdoms of day and 12 kingdoms of night.

The Day

As he moves throughout his journey, Ra undergoes three transformations:

  • Khepri

    Represented as a scarab beetle, Khepri symbolizes the newly-born sun of dawn in the mornings.

  • Ra

    In the afternoons, he becomes a young man with a falcon head. This is the form most people know.

  • Atum

    In the evening, he transforms into Atum - an old man with a cane.

Throughout the day, he travels through 12 kingdoms of solar deities.

At the end of the day, he is swallowed by the sky goddess Nut and travels through the Underworld to be reborn again.

The Night

The Afterlife is divided into 12 regions, each guarded by a gate. As the day turns to night and Ra lays dead, his body is taken care of the 12 goddesses of the night, as well as other deities.

Ra: The Sun God ☀︎

The sun god Ra was ancient Egypt's most revered and sacred deity. He was worshipped as the king of the gods and the father of creation.

note: Although Ra is the main sun god, other contenders for the title of Egyptian sun god includes:

  • Aten the sun disk, who became more worshipped than Ra under the rule of Akhenaten.

  • Amun, who wasn't a sun god until he was fused with Ra to become Amun-Ra.

  • Horus, who was a sky god that was, on occasion, fused with Ra as well.

His human form was normally depicted with the head of a falcon wearing a sun disc. Other symbols included:

  • a scarab beatle
  • a ram
  • the sun itself

Ra Spawned All Life

According to many Egyptian myths, Ra created all life.

Out of eternal darkness, and entity called Atum called forth the beginning of creation. An island emerged out of the Primeval Ocean, which turned into Ra, the sun god. Ra spawned the first gods: Shu, Tefnut, Geb, and Nut.

Geb and Nut would go on to birth Osiris, the Perfect Being, who became ruler of the world.

According to Egyptian myth, Ra created the elements and produced humans from his tears. As the creator of the universe, Ra became father and king to all gods, humans, and living creatures.

The Eye of Ra

The eye of Ra, not to be confused with Ra himself, was the god's feminine counterpart - sometimes described as his daughter, wife, or mother. It was an extention of Ra's power that was associated with several goddesses including Bastet, Ma'at, and Sekhmet, each of which had a role in protecting Ra from external evil forces.
